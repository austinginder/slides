Someone Bought 30 Plugins
and Planted a Backdoor

A malicious actor purchased the Essential Plugin portfolio on Flippa for six figures in early 2025 and injected backdoors into all of them.

anchor.host → full write-up

How It Happened

Early 2025

Portfolio purchased on Flippa by "Kris" — backgrounds in SEO, crypto, and gambling marketing

8 Months

Backdoor injected but remained dormant across 30+ plugins

April 2026

Attack activated — SEO spam and hidden content distributed via blockchain-based C2 servers

Discovered

Found by Austin Ginder of Anchor Hosting

Impact & Takeaways

31 Plugins Closed

Permanently removed from WordPress.org including Countdown Timer Ultimate, Popup Anything on Click, WP Testimonial with Widget, and more

Ecosystem Gap

WordPress.org has no mechanism to flag or review plugin ownership transfers

What site owners should do

  • Audit installations for affected plugin slugs
  • Inspect wp-config.php for injected backdoor code
  • Remove or replace any of the 31 closed plugins

"What WordPress would be if it was created in 2026"

wpproducttalk.com → full episode

The Two Matts from Cloudflare

Matt "TK" Taylor

Senior Product Manager

Decade of publishing and media experience. Previously at Financial Times and News Corp (a major WordPress customer).

Matt Kane

Senior Principal Engineer

Core maintainer of the Astro web framework for ~2 years.

What Makes EmDash Different

  • Plugin sandboxing — granular, specified capabilities instead of unrestricted access
  • Built-in SEO & roles — core features, not plugin-dependent
  • Modern stack — Astro framework + Node.js
  • AI-native design — emphasizing developer experience and AI interaction
  • MIT licensed — not GPL
  • Portable — runs on Cloudflare Workers, AWS Lambda, Netlify, Vercel

Opportunity or Threat?

Opportunity

  • "Gold rush" for early plugin developers
  • First-mover advantage in a new ecosystem
  • Flexible monetization with MIT licensing
  • Open-source, community-driven governance

Threat

  • Addresses real WordPress security pain points
  • Modern dev practices attract new developers
  • Faster development cycles
  • AI-native workflows vs WordPress legacy
